Lucid Drum

Stanford TreeHacks 2020 - Most Creative Award
Role (team of 4): Game Design, UX Design, VR development
Tools: Unity, Figma, Oculus Quest

The Challenge
The physical world is no longer the end-all-be-all of human experiences. The AR/VR challenge pushes hackers to create their own realities and the tools necessary to experience them in order to tackle a breadth of problems and push the boundaries of human experiences.

The Solution
Lucid Drums is an Oculus Quest Game in which we generate a fantastic virtual reality environment that enables children to explore music freely and rhythmically. It helps children learn different kinds of rhythms by playing drums to music in a VR world. We use machine learning to generate rhythms to songs, leverage hand-tracking to capture real-time movement, and apply game UX and learning principles to engage users.

Chimera

How might we prepare high school and college students who are entering the workplace for the essencial empathy and collaboration skills?
Role (team of 2): Game Design, UX Design, Programming
Tools: Unity, Figma, Blender

The Need
Currently, 86% of professionals indicate a lack of collaboration to be the main cause of workplace failures. And studies show that levels of empathy in the population have been dropping. This is concerning because, in today's professional environments, there is an increasing demand for employees that can skillfully navigate group dynamics.

The Solution
Chimera is a secretly educational video game that motivates players to build leadership, collaboration, and empathy skills through interacting with a dynamic and responsive world. As players engage with the world of Chimera, they develop and iterate on necessary skills in a challenging environment that simulates the professional workplace.

Project is still under development

Food Challenge VR

How might we reduce patients' anxiety during the Oral Food Challenge procedure for food allergy testing?
Role (team of 2): UX Design, Programming
Tools: Unity, Oculus Quest, Pico Neo
Project partners: Stanford Virtual Human Interaction Lab & Sean N. Parker Center for Allergy and Asthma Research

The Need
The Food Challenge is a medical procedure in which a potential allergen is eaten in gradually increasing amounts over time to accurately diagnose or rule out a true food allergy. Anxiety during the exam can elicit symptoms that can be confused with allergic reactions, especially among young children. The goal of the VR application is to minimize distress during the procedure to improve procedural efficiency and the experience of younger patients, ages 6 to 12.

The Solution
To address the issue, we created a VR experience in which patients are empowered with magical powers upon eating the doses, giving them magical powers to restore the forest.

Phonics Tangram

How might we make the learning of phonics more engaging and immersive for young learners?
Solo project
Tools: Unity, Figma, Vuforia
Project partners: Kindergarten teacher

The Need
Phonics can be hard to understand as it lacks visual representation. A kindergarten teacher reached out to me and as if there are innovative ways to implement some of the existing practices using the power of mixed reality and increasing students' engagement.

The Solution
With the teacher sharing her experiences of teaching early-year English, I built an AR application that implements these practices. The application utilizes iPads and alphabetical puzzle pieces, which enable students to visually and auditorily understand English pronunciation when the letter pieces form a word.

Makery Tour

How might we facilitate a self-guided tour for newcomers to understand the tools and spark their interest in making?
Solo project
Tools: Unity, Figma, AR Core, Blender
Project partners: Stanford GSE Makery

The Need
Makerspaces can be overwhelming because of the mass amount of tools that are rarely seen elsewhere. This creates an invisible barrier for newcomers to understand the equipment before even starting to create.

The Solution
Using augmented reality, I created an application that allows users to navigate through the makerspace as they learn more about each tool. The indicators show points of interest, such as machines or workstations, and users can click on them to get more detailed information as well as pictures of previous projects in a carousel to get inspiration.

Pandemonium

How might we design a board game that battles the wide spread of disinformation?
Role (team of 4): Game Design, Artwork
Tools: Figma, Paper prototype
Project partners: Stanford Virtual Human Interaction Lab & Sean N. Parker Center for Allergy and Asthma Research

The Need
Media literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, and create media in a variety of forms. However, there are increasing amounts of misinformation and disinformation spreading online.

The Solution
Pandemonium is an educational game modded off The Resistance: Avalon aiming to help players gain media literacy by learning to differentiate Informational news from Disinformation. The players are either Good reporters who try to distinguish informational news from disinformation, or Evil deceivers who try to spread the lies and confuse the other readers.

The game was also recently translated to Spanish to battle against the spread of disinformation related to COVID-19.
